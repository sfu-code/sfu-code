Details

As this site is meant to be the single source of truth for information about SFU Code, it will be continually updated with event information, projects, etc. All club communications are via Slack.

Culture

The atmosphere at SFU Code is hyper-collaborative: anybody is welcome to pitch an idea, suggest an improvement, or just take matters into their own hands and do something. Anyone is welcome to join SFU Code and drop in to meetings or seminars. If you want to find some teammates to build with or want to learn something new, you have come to the right place.

Get Involved

If you would like to start a project on behalf of SFU Code, like a developer tool, an SFU-themed app, or some other open-source software, feel free to create a repository in our organization. This way, you can add collaborators to the repo, others can submit pull requests, and the project will be presented as a product of SFU Code. Or, you can always create a personal repo and have us fork it.

Club Mandate

To bring together students interested in the design and development of digital products and services, fostering a community of practice where students can learn from others and share their own knowledge.
